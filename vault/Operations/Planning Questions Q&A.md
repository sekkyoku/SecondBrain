**Q5. Optimization levers for performance in lieu of MMP / pixel tracking?**

**Q6. If a BLS is implemented, would dark periods impact measurement? Best practices?**
Yes, dark periods can impact BLS measurements — they interrupt consistent exposure delivery and survey sampling stability.

**Best practices:**
- Maintain a minimum always-on delivery if possible to keep consistent exposure volume and survey sample flow
- If planning a dark period, ideally keep within **1–3 days** to avoid disruption to survey sample flow

**Q7. How often can reach & frequency reporting be provided?**
For CTV, R&F reporting can be provided **as needed** and can be scheduled.

**Q8. For site-served reporting, can you provide data weekly?**
**Yes** — site-served reporting can be provided weekly, but must be **flagged prior to campaign launch**.

**Q9. Can you provide ad hoc analysis (incremental reach via ACR, search lift, ROAS, etc.)?**
Can provide **incremental reach via CAS data**. Campaign would need iSpot pixel appended to CTV lines and must meet **minimum iSpot spend threshold** (check with sales).

**Q10. For programmatic media, is data/targeting decoupled or must it run with a deal?**
**It must run with a deal.**

## Key Takeaways

- **Minimums:** $25K for both DIO and programmatic
- **Added value requires IO** — not available via programmatic alone
- **BLS threshold:** $500K + 6 weeks + 7MM impressions
- **Retargeting:** DIO only recommended; pixel must be placed at campaign start; mobile/desktop only
- **Data/targeting must run with a deal** — not decoupled
- **Upweight/downweight media:** DIO only
- **Max video length:** :30s (OLV supports :60s+ but shorter is preferred)
- **Attitudinal/attention pixels:** Not previously used by Canela — needs evaluation
