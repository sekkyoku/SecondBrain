# Programmatic Guaranteed (PG)

## Deal Mechanics

- **Serving:** Third-party served
- **Inventory:** Programmatic
- **CPM:** Fixed
- **Budget Minimums:** None, but availability must be confirmed prior to campaign start
- **Setup:** Direct integration between Canela platform and DSP

## How It Works

PG deals are executed via a direct integration between the Canela platform and the buyer's DSP. Targeting parameters are typically locked at the time of deal setup. Minor adjustments may be possible, but significant changes require deal reconfiguration and re-approval.

## Frequency & Delivery

- Frequency caps supported (but **not recommended** for scale and deliver-in-full)
- Manual frequency caps accepted (but **not recommended** for scale)
- No additional bid/budget management needed (fixed commitment)

## Known DSP Conflicts

- Inventory access via **TTD (The Trade Desk)** can be more limited compared to other DSPs — may impact scale and delivery
- Certain DSP settings (creative duration mismatches, unsupported formats, strict third-party tracking) may impact delivery
- Overly restrictive targeting layers, frequency caps, or conflicting audience settings may limit scale

## Comparison vs [[Private Marketplace (PMP)]]

| Dimension | PG | PMP |
|-----------|----|----|
| CPM | Fixed | Dynamic (auction) |
| Commitment | Guaranteed delivery | Bid-to-win |
| Targeting flexibility | Locked at setup | Adjustable in DSP |
| Budget risk | Availability-dependent | Bid-competitive |
| VideoAmp pixel | Yes | No |
| Scale risk | DSP compatibility | Bid threshold + targeting layers |
