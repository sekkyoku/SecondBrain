# Private Marketplace (PMP)

## Deal Mechanics

- **Serving:** Third-party served
- **Inventory:** Programmatic
- **CPM:** Dynamic (auction-based)
- **Budget Minimums:** No strict minimums; sufficient budget recommended to remain competitive
- **Setup:** Deal IDs shared with buyer, activated within DSP

## How It Works

PMP deals are set up via Deal IDs shared with the buyer and activated within the DSP. Buyers apply targeting, bidding, and frequency settings directly in-platform. Inventory access is immediate upon deal activation.

## Bid & Budget Management

Performance and delivery are influenced by bid competitiveness, targeting layers, and frequency settings. Bid adjustments may be required to optimize scale and win rates throughout the campaign.

## Frequency & Delivery

- Frequency caps managed within the DSP, customizable by campaign objective and inventory type
- Manual frequency caps accepted and adjustable within the DSP

## Known DSP Conflicts

- Overly restrictive targeting, low bid thresholds, or aggressive frequency caps may limit scale and reduce win rates
- Creative mismatches (duration, file size, tracking requirements) affect eligibility and scale
- Overly restrictive verification settings or misaligned VAST configurations may limit delivery

## Key Difference: VideoAmp

> **PMP does NOT accept VideoAmp pixel.** Measurement supported via DSP reporting, server-to-server integrations, and alternative attribution solutions.

This is a critical difference from [[Programmatic Guaranteed (PG)]] which does accept VideoAmp.

## IAS Best Practices (PMP-specific)

Leverage IAS pre-bid segments for brand safety, suitability, and IVT filtering. Balance targeting layers to maintain scale while ensuring quality. The fewer exclusions, the better the scale.
